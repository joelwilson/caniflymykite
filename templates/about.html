The idea for Can I Fly My Kite came to us one day from the sad notion that, should the urge to fly a kite arise while in-doors, it is difficult or, at the very least, cumbersome to quickly determine if the weather outside is condusive to the flying of kites.

Of course there are snazzy, feature-rich, and fully loaded web services out there that will begrudgingly give out weather data, and we tip our hat to anyone who can navigate the horror that is weather.com, but good luck trying to get any of those services to translate raw weather data in to kite-flyingness (not a word).

This is the essence of Can I Fly My Kite: to be the translator of weather info for kite flyers of the world. We take the weather information for your location and provide a simple yes or no answer, immediately answering the nagging question that kite enthusiasts often ponder:

Can I Fly My Kite?
